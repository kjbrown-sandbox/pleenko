# Steam Trailer Screens

## 1. "The First Drop" (0:00–0:05)
**Single coin, tiny 2-row board.** Let it bounce and land. Simple, clean, immediately readable. The viewer understands the game in 2 seconds. Hold on the bucket pulse + gold number ticking up.

*Why it works:* Plinko is universally understood. Zero explanation needed.

## 2. "The Board Grows" (0:05–0:12)
**Time-lapse the board expanding** — cut between 2 rows, 6 rows, 10 rows, 14 rows. Each cut shows more pegs, wider buckets, bigger numbers. The camera smoothly reframes each time (you already have the tween for this).

*Why it works:* Shows progression at a glance. The board literally getting bigger is satisfying and communicates "things scale up."

## 3. "The Flood" (0:12–0:20)
**Max drop rate + autodroppers + queue full.** Show coins pouring down a large board at 5+ per second. Multiple coins bouncing simultaneously, bucket pulses firing constantly, floating text stacking up. This is your spectacle shot — let it run for several seconds.

*Why it works:* The #1 thing idle game players want to see is "big numbers going fast." A board full of bouncing coins is inherently mesmerizing.

## 4. "The Multiplier Hit" (0:20–0:25)
**Close-up on an advanced coin (3x multiplier) landing in a high-value edge bucket.** The floating "x3 = 450" text pops up, the bucket flashes, the currency bar jumps. Maybe a multi-drop with the "x2" billboard visible above the drop point.

*Why it works:* Shows depth beyond basic drops. Multipliers = dopamine. The floating text makes the math feel rewarding.

## 5. "Three Boards" (0:25–0:33)
**Camera pulls back to show all three boards side by side** — Gold, Orange, Red. Each with its own color palette and activity. Cut to the keyboard arrows switching between them, camera tweening smoothly. Show the tier colors clearly (gold → orange → red progression).

*Why it works:* Communicates that this isn't just one board forever. Three distinct economies = long-term engagement. The color coding is immediately legible.

## 6. "The Prestige Moment" (0:33–0:38)
**Prestige dialog appearing.** Show the multi-drop bonus unlocking (+1 coin per drop on lower boards), the new board lighting up. This is your "one more run" hook — the moment where everything resets but you're stronger.

*Why it works:* Idle game veterans know what prestige means. Showing it confirms there's real depth.

## 7. "Theme Swap" (0:38–0:42)
**Quick cuts between your three visual themes** — warm dark halo, warm minimal, cool slate. Same board state, different vibes. Shows polish and personality.

*Why it works:* Signals quality. Players notice when a game has visual options. It also makes the trailer itself more visually varied.

## 8. "The Upgrade Path" (0:42–0:48)
**Quick montage of buying upgrades.** Fill bars completing, buttons pulsing, new features appearing (queue slots populating, autodropper buttons showing up, advanced buckets appearing at board edges). Show the upgrade rows with costs ticking up.

*Why it works:* The upgrade panel is the decision-making layer. Showing it communicates "this isn't just watching — you're making choices."

## 9. "Zen Mode" Closer (0:48–0:55)
**End on a fully upgraded board with coins flowing.** No UI — just the 3D board, pegs, and coins bouncing. Let the satisfying physics speak. Maybe cycle through themes slowly. Fade to title/logo.

*Why it works:* Idle games sell on vibes. End with the feeling of "I could watch this forever."

## Production Notes

- **Total runtime: ~55 seconds.** Short enough for Steam's autoplay, long enough to show depth.
- **No voiceover needed.** Minimal text overlays ("Drop." / "Grow." / "Prestige." / "Repeat.") would work well with your minimalist aesthetic.
- **Music:** A chill lo-fi or synthwave track would match the clean 3D look. Steam allows you to add a soundtrack DLC later if you find something good.
- **Capture method:** Godot's `--fixed-fps 60` flag + screen recording. Stage each shot in a debug save state so you can get the exact board configuration you want.
- **Your biggest visual asset is the coin flood on a large board.** That should be the thumbnail/capsule image too. A 14-row board with 20 coins mid-bounce, bright colors against a dark background — that's your store page hero shot.
